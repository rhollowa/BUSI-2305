Course Description

BUSI 2305 introduces statistical methods used in business decision-making. Students learn to summarize data, estimate population characteristics, test hypotheses, and build simple predictive models. The course emphasizes interpretation over computation — the goal is to read a chart, a study, or a report and know whether the conclusion is defensible.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Summarize and visualize datasets using descriptive statistics and standard chart types
  • Apply concepts of probability and sampling to business contexts
  • Construct and interpret confidence intervals
  • Conduct hypothesis tests and explain the meaning of statistical significance
  • Fit and interpret simple linear regression models
  • Evaluate the quality of statistical claims in business reporting

Required Materials

All required readings and lecture videos are provided through this site at no cost. A spreadsheet program (Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers) is required for assignments.

Grading

  • Weekly problem sets — 40%
  • Two midterm exams — 30%
  • Final project — 20%
  • Participation in office hours or discussion board — 10%

Communication

Email is the fastest way to reach the instructor. For substantive questions about course material, please bring them to office hours when possible — most questions benefit other students too.

What to Expect

Office hours are informal. Show up with your camera on or off, with your name or a question, with everything ready or with nothing prepared. The standing format is:

  • First 15 minutes: walk through anything from the current week's lecture students want clarified
  • Middle 30 minutes: open Q&A on problem sets, exam prep, or general statistical thinking
  • Last 15 minutes: bring your own dataset / project for live workshopping

If you can't make either session, send an email and we'll find another time.
